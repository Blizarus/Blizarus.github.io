This insignificant episode did not have the slightest effect on the wave of excitement caused by the transfer student in the class.
Han Li, who was sitting in the last row, remained calm, seemingly unaffected by the noise around him.
His figure half-tilted to the side, and his slender fingers with clear joints were turning a pen slowly. The dazzling sunlight came in from outside the half-covered curtains, the deep shadows of his eyelashes cast on his pale profile, and his thin lips were pursed into an inhumane line of indifference.
Han Li narrowed his eyes and stared at the slightly arched and thin back of the young man not far away, the slightly hooked corners of his lips, and his gaze fixed on the transfer student.
“Clack.”
The pen in his hand slipped out of his hands, rolled around on the table, and finally stopped between the gaps in the table.
Han Li lowered his eyes, looking at the pen that was stuck between the narrow gap of the table and the other table, a faint hint of hostility gathering in the depths of his deep and quiet eyes.
The bell for the end of class rang.
Han Li picked up a few exercise booklets that had been placed on his table and stood up. Before he could even take a few steps, he was stopped by a soft voice:
“…Classmate, are you okay?”
He turned his head and looked in the direction where the sound came from, only to see the transfer student sitting in his seat, staring at him worriedly with a pair of round cat eyes. The sincerity in his eyes were undisguised, and his delicate and beautiful face seemed to glow in the sunlight, as if no matter how reckless his actions were, he could be easily forgiven.
Han Li impatiently frowned and glanced at him coldly. He did not answer and just limped forward.
Ye Jinghuan felt a little bit lost. He rarely experienced being directly ignored by others like this, and he couldn’t help but feel somewhat aggrieved.
The deskmate sitting next to him noticed his strange mood, came over, and asked, “What’s wrong?”
Ye Jinghuan shook his head uneasily, raised his eyes to look at Han Li’s retreating figure, and subconsciously bit his lip: “Does he… not like me very much?”
His female deskmate, overwhelmed by his expression, quickly shook her head: “No, no, no, how could it be? Han Li is just different. He basically treats everyone this way. He’s not intentionally targeting you, so don’t take it to heart.”
Ye Jinghuan was dumbfounded for a moment: “…Different?”
His deskmate pretended to be mysterious and lowered her voice, saying: “I actually heard what others said. He seems to have a very deep background. He doesn’t come to school often, but his academic performance has never been too bad. I’ve seen many people in the class, even including the teachers, seem a bit afraid of him. He’s quite handsome, and many girls have a crush on him, but not many of them really dare to confess to him. The only pity is his legs…”
“Legs?” Ye Jinghuan’s curiosity was piqued, prompting him to question closely.
