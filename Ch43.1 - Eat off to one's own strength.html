“Yes, yes, I’m most terrified of snakes.”

His tone was sincere. His amber eyes, glinting innocently in the dappled forest light, made it almost impossible to doubt the truth of his words.

The instructor, misled by his demeanor and words, also followed along and said:

Shen Kong nodded vigorously and took the small knife from the instructor’s hand, feeling slightly relieved.

——Aside from the two instructors, no one present had any wilderness survival experience, let alone the ability to judge whether the angle and force with which the knife hit the tree stump were professional. So as long as the instructor framed the incident as a burst of adrenaline-fueled potential, the others wouldn’t question it further. However, what Shen Kong wasn’t sure about was whether any camera had recorded the entire scene. After all, among the thousands of viewers, there might be a few who could recognize the truth, not to mention President Han, who was overseeing everything.

Seeing that the most experienced instructor had spoken, the others indeed did not dwell on the topic any further. After reiterating the safety precautions three more times, the group set off again along the predetermined route.

Shen Kong, carrying his backpack, walked at the end of the line while pondering how to approach the production team later.

However, he had only taken a few steps when he felt a gentle tug on his sleeve.

He looked in the direction of the tug and saw Ran Wan walking beside him. She had recovered from her earlier fright. A flush of excitement now colored her small, fair face. The resentment and grievance from before seemed to have vanished. Her bright, beautiful eyes watched him shyly.

She pursed her lips and whispered:
